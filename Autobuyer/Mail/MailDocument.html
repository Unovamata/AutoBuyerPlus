Microsoft Defender AutoBuyer+ Trojan Detection

Thank you for your continuous support in the development of AutoBuyer+.

Microsoft Defender has issued a false-positive with the release of AutoBuyer+ 0.8v. On March 29th, a couple of users reported AutoBuyer+ being flagged as a Trojan:HTML/Obfuse.JSI virus.

To be clear, AutoBuyer+ is not a trojan nor a virus. From the start, we have developed AutoBuyer+ with our users' privacy and integrity at the front, we have been quite vocal about this throughout development.

 

 

Microsoft Defender mistakenly detected AutoBuyer+ because of these 3 reasons:

  1. AutoBuyer+ is an automation tool: Because AutoBuyer+ is sending data to Neopets' servers to automate purchases, this prompted Windows Defender to think that AutoBuyer+ may be sending sensitive data to third-party servers.
    1. Why is this not the case? The file flagged as a Trojan is the "AutoHaggler" process file, which coincidentally, it's the only file that sends data in this format to Neopets' servers, more on this later.
    2. What are the developers going to do about this? We have deofuscated the script that was causing this issue.
  2. AutoBuyer+ was heavily obfuscated on version 0.8v: You may remember that some time ago, we discussed through a mail about a user that reported AutoBuyer+ to TNT's staff members in a Neopets Facebook group. 
    1. Why did we take this decision? TNT could easily deploy systems to detect the extension based on its structure, obfuscating AutoBuyer+ was the only way of protecting our users in the long run.
    2. Why didn't you take this decision sooner? It wasn't necessary at the time, Neopets has become popular in the past year or so. AutoBuyer+ has been in development for almost 3 years now, it wasn't a pressing concern back when the site was on JumpStart's hands.
  3. Microsoft Defender is one of the worst Anti-Viruses out there: While useful for everyday tasks, for something as process intensive, demanding, and complex as automating/botting, Microsoft Defender will suspect about processes like these  without taking into account this is a Chrome Extension.
    1. Why do you say Microsoft Defender is a bad Anti-Virus? Do you have any proof? Yes, please check this Virus Total Scan. AutoBuyer+ was tested against 62 Anti-Virus programs, where none of them detected the extension as a threat, including Microsoft's propietary Anti-Virus.
    2. Why would Microsoft Defender Detect AutoBuyer+ as a Trojan? Because of the reasons mentioned above and AutoBuyer+ takes some control away from the user. Even though it's inoffensive, you have to accept AutoBuyer+ clicks, opens, and manages information inside a contained browser environment.

 

 

  • Why should I trust AutoBuyer+ after this?
    • You can decide to opt-out from using AutoBuyer+, we will completely understand your decision if so. If anything, it will help us improve in future releases to earn your trust back.
    • We wanted to be transparent, we could have swept this under the rug and ignore the handful of users experiencing this issue and prompting a quick fix, but our audience doesn't deserve that treatment.
    • As we have said many times, we are compromised on protecting your accounts, your privacy, and time. We do not condone acccount hacking nor black market shenannigans.
    • We have deofuscated the file that caused the conflict and we're more than willing to deofuscate more files that may cause similar issues in the future.

 

  • Can AutoBuyer+ take over my computer or my accounts & side-accounts?
    • No, Google Chrome extensions run in a contained environment. These tools cannot read, modify, or change information in your computer. Executing malicious code is out of the question.
    • Chromium environments do not allow for much administrative power over the user's machine, so it's close to impossible to cause any harm. Else Google Chrome's Extension Store would be plagued with viruses and trojans.

 

  • Well, what now? 
    • First of all, thank you for reading up to this point. We appreciate your understanding, time, and support. At the end of the day, we're just hobbyists trying to automate a very repetitive and grindy MMO.
    • Please, leave everything to us, we understand that earning your trust back won't be easy, but we are compromised to protect you as our end-user as much as humanly possible.
    • We don't promise this won't happen again because of the nature of AutoBuyer+, but we will try to minimize any negative impact the extension might provoke. We didn't know this could happen and weren't prepared for it, we deeply apologize for any concern.
